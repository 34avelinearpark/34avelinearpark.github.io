Our Petition

Support for a Linear Park in Jackson Heights


Since the launch of our petition in May 2021, over 2,500 people have signed on in support of a permanent park on 34th Avenue, currently home to one of the city's most successful Open Streets. As our community recovers from the Covid-19 pandemic, the potential for new, permanent greenspace in Jackson Heights has activated the imagination of our neighbors and fellow open space advocates and energized a discussion about environmental equity. A selection of the comments submitted to us through the petition are shared below.

Some comments were edited for length.


x Just as Travers Park has become a focal place for community, so has 34th Avenue brought residents, visitors, and those who work in the neighborhood together. As we look to the future, innovative infrastructural and design changes are needed to sustain + improve upon the success of the all-volunteer Open Streets program.
x We are a community of both great diversity and immense disparity. In 2020, we were in the epicenter of the Covid-19 epicenter. As we grapple with a public health emergency like none other in recent history, existing inequities are all the more pronounced. Good open spaces are one just component to achieving a more equitable city.
x JH ranks among the lowest in per capita green space. Uniquely, unlike many of the city's Open Streets, 34th Avenue is residential. The Jackson Heights community has made the 34th Avenue Open Street its very own with personal touches ranging from gardens to activity spaces for all ages. But there's still work to be done to meet the gap in open space.
x For every user of our current Open Street, there is a different vision for what permanent green space in Jackson Heights could look like. We strongly believe that the City should adopt a community-based approach to 34th Avenue that builds upon the work and successes of the community, which helped harness the potential in these 26 blocks.

We are Friends of 34th Avenue Linear Park, a collective of Jackson Heights residents from different backgrounds and different parts of the neighborhood. Among us are public school parents, students, immigrants, residents of 34th Avenue, and people who have called Jackson Heights home for their entire lives. We have banded together as volunteer organizers for a common cause:

To support the creation of a linear park on 34th Avenue.
